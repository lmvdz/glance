# Design: the-room — a buzz-shaped collaborative chat workspace as the product's root, with glance/t3code depth surfaces as doors off every card

FINAL — adversarial round 2 arbitration, 2026-07-22. Supersedes DESIGN-DRAFT.md. Binding inputs: LANDSCAPE.md (incl. §Binding directives from Lars), REDTEAM-A.md, REDTEAM-B.md, buzz-borrows DESIGN.md verdicts.

## Approach

Build the full application in the webapp: a new root route (HubShell) that owns the whole viewport — channel rail, card timeline, composer — with today's workbench views demoted to layer-2 modes reached through cards. Layer 1 is genuinely multiplayer (per-human identity at the socket, durable human messages, membership the transport actually enforces) and genuinely a control plane (the composer steers and spawns agents, with ack/nack, from day one of the visible increment — never a read-only feed). Cards are manager-authored proofs projected from units into channels by an explicit routing rule; every card kind lands together with its reader/door, and every door action emits a card back into the room. Human messages get a durable store (DB rows in DB mode); re-derivable projections may stay lossy. glance-desktop is superseded outright (unused, broken — Lars's directive); the t3-face love-gate protocol re-targets to this room, run against the whole experience. Nothing is deferred to imaginary later phases; landing order alone carries the discipline.

## Program shape

Full application, five waves. Each wave = independently landable, reviewable units; standard gauntlet (blind review + cross-lineage on trust/tenancy/git-write paths) per unit.

- **Wave 0 — daemon + identity substrate** (invisible; unthrottled; internal tracks parallelize):
  - Channel store: DB rows via store seam + JsonlLog hot tail; file-mode JSONL hardening + shutdown flush; per-channel seq/cursor; redact chokepoint; born-settled test; authorship rule (event-field strip/reject) + test.
  - WS identity: session-at-upgrade, SocketData userId/displayName; presence from per-user socket sets; file-mode operator identity stated.
  - Ack/nack: command-ack event arm + silent-drop fix (daemon) + clientTurnId minting reversal (webapp lib).
  - Projection: channelId-at-spawn plumbing, #fleet default, routing rule, structured ValidationRecord emit at verify-before-land, unit→channel pointer-card projection with neutralize+redact.
  - Landable/reviewable: each track is its own PR with tests; nothing renders yet, so no love-gate exposure.
- **Wave 1 — HubShell root** (first Lars-visible frame): root route owning the viewport — channel rail, timeline (typed-card renderer bound to channel entries), hub composer; hash router; workbench views mounted as routes behind it (reachable, demoted). Cold-boot lands in #fleet. Checkpoint review with Lars on the first frame (informal, pre-gate).
- **Wave 2 — the room as control plane** (partially parallel with wave 3): human↔human messages (durable), presence, mention grammar (steer w/ ack, working-agent queue-or-confirm, spawn-proposal from chat — SpawnProposal/ConfirmSheet relocated into the room), replies (replyToId + thread view), channel search over durable rows. This wave is what makes the increment a chat workspace, not a feed (B-F1): the composer verbs land here, before any love-gate run.
- **Wave 3 — doors** (kind+reader units, ordered): needs-you→IntervenceView (flagship, live-by-construction) → gate-verdict + GateVerdictCard + historical proof endpoint/post-mortem mode → plan-card→plan-DAG editor → token-burn-snapshot→fleet economics → land-attempt/assessment/merge → design-revised → return-emit on every door action. Each door tested against a landed-and-removed unit, not just a running one (B-F4).
- **Wave 4 — multiplayer hardening**: membership + per-channel fan-out filter (one unit, with leak tests), presence fidelity (typing indicators), concurrent-steer visibility polish, read cursors, second-human live smoke (two browsers, two identities, DB mode).
- **Parallel throughout**: ACP spike (time-boxed); t3-face craft-harvest pass (feeds wave 1–3 card polish); daily-driver convergence (`glance here` threads in rail, push-latch wiring) rides waves 2–3.

## The love gate

Adapted from t3-face 13, re-targeted at the webapp room (directive 2, B-F2/F11). Runs when waves 1–3 are landed and wave 4's org-public path works — i.e., against the **whole room experience**, not a single card demo. Protocol: scratch-daemon with real seeded fleet data; Lars cold-boots the URL. Audited axes, falsifiable: (1) cold-boot first frame — does it read as the product's home, not a panel in a dashboard; (2) the rail — channels + active work legible at a glance; (3) the timeline — cards as proofs, scannable, no firehose burying human messages; (4) the composer — address an agent, get an ack, spin one up, all from chat; (5) the doors — click a card into depth and back, including one dead/historical door that degrades honestly. Lars's reaction is the real gate; no wave-5 feature work on new surfaces until it passes. Checkpoint (non-gate) reviews after wave 1 and wave 2 de-risk the final run.

## DIRECTION.md amendment (draft for Lars)

> ### Two-layer architecture (supersedes prior single-surface framing)
>
> The product is two layers. **Layer 1 is the room**: a buzz-shaped collaborative chat workspace — org-scoped channels where humans and agents are peers, browser-delivered, multiplayer by default. It is the home screen; users spin up and steer agents from chat and never have to leave it.
>
> **Layer 2 is depth, entered from chat, never the home screen.** glance's depth: plan/workflow DAG, design revision, fleet economics. t3code's depth: the programmer's view — active/waiting agents, transcripts, diffs, steer. Every layer-2 *event* projects a card into the room; the room's rail (channels + active work) is a **legitimate standing entrance** to depth surfaces — cards are the narrative way in, the rail is the standing way in. *(Open for your ratification: the earlier, stronger form — "a card is the only way in" — is deliberately not adopted; it would force scroll-hunting for the latest card to reach the programmer view.)*
>
> **Layer 2 never happens silently**: any action in a depth surface emits a card back into the room. The room is the complete **live** projection of system state; history is durable in DB mode (channel rows), best-effort in single-operator file mode.
>
> **Cards are proofs, not agent self-reports** — gate verdicts, land assessments, done-proofs from the trust layer; event-bearing cards are manager-authored only and unforgeable by clients. This is the differentiation; the daemon brain is not rebuilt.
>
> **t3code is repositioned, not deprecated**: its thread grammar and card craft are absorbed into layer 1; "t3code" names the programmer lens. The terax law generalizes: every expert surface is a mode in one shell. **glance-desktop is superseded** (unused, non-working); t3-face visual work is harvested as design input via a dedicated craft pass; hub-shell is closed with a pointer here.
>
> **Multiplayer is in scope from the start**: per-human identity, durable messages, presence, membership with transport-enforced visibility.
>
> **Sequencing call reserved for you**: the daily-driver program's arbitrated entry surface is terminal-first; the-room is browser-first. This design converges them (needs-you cards ride the push latch; `glance here` threads appear in the room's rail) but whether the-room's waves queue ahead of or behind the daily-driver adoption gate is your call, made explicitly, not by omission.

## Open Questions — RESOLVED by Lars, 2026-07-22 (design gate)

1. **Sequencing vs daily-driver**: **Room leads.** The-room waves run now as the primary program; daily-driver converges into it — its threads and push lane become room projections. The room is the daily driver's destination.
